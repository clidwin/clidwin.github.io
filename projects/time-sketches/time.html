How might we animate aspects of time?

Using the seconds, minutes, and hours of time, I created a clock that focuses on visual (rather than textual or hand-based) elements.
View Clock

Client

In Fall 2014, I created this piece as a part of a course on Processing. There was no topic for the assignment, but I wanted to explore how to code time-based animations. After the course, I started working on turning this to an Android Watch face.

Inspiration

I was interested in the idea of exploring the use of circles (or the illusion of circular shapes) to create a piece with meaning. This was combined with an idea I had to create a watch that did not have hands or numbers.

Languages and Technologies

This time animation uses the following technologies:
HTML5 & CSS3
p5.js

How It Works

Using Processing's javascript library, the animation draws thirty frames a second based on the current time. When the seconds variable in Processing is zero, animations occur and spark other animations based on whether the minutes or hours is also zero. At midnight, the clock starts with nothing filled in and the shapes fill in in a clockwise manner as time passes.
